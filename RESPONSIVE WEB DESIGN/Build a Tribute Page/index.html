Tina Turner is celebrated as the "Queen of Rock 'n' Roll" for her iconic career and resilience.

Tina Turner during a photo shoot for "Proud Mary" in the 1970s.

Here is a time line of Tina Turners life

  • 1939 - Tina Turner (born Anna Mae Bullock) is born on November 26th in Nutbush, Tennessee, USA.
  • 1958 - She meets Ike Turner and starts performing with his band, the Kings of Rhythm.
  • 1960 - Tina and Ike Turner release "A Fool in Love," which becomes their first major hit.
  • 1962 - The duo releases "Proud Mary," one of their most iconic songs.
  • 1966 - Ike & Tina Turner's "River Deep - Mountain High," which peaked at #88 in America, climbs to #3 in the UK, earning them a gig opening for The Rolling Stones. Their success has tragic consequences, as Ike gets more an more violent toward Tina, who endures years of abuse.
  • 1976 - Tina files for divorce from Ike Turner, ending their tumultuous marriage and professional partnership.
  • 1984 - Tina Turner releases her solo album "Private Dancer," which includes hits like "What's Love Got to Do with It."
  • 1985 - She wins multiple Grammy Awards, including Record of the Year for "What's Love Got to Do with It."
  • 1993 - Tina publishes her autobiography, "I, Tina," which details her life, career, and struggles.
  • 2008 - Tina Turner embarks on her "Tina!: 50th Anniversary Tour," marking five decades in the music industry.
  • 2013 - She becomes a Swiss citizen, having lived in Switzerland for several years.
  • 2021 - Tina Turner's life and career are celebrated in the documentary "Tina," which provides an intimate look at her journey.
  • 2023 - Tina Turner dies at 83 after a long illness.
